Methodology

How this catalogue is built, and how "independent" is defined.

IndependentWatchmaking.com is a free, independently curated reference documenting the full spectrum of independent horology worldwide — legendary masters, AHCI members, one-person artisan workshops, historic manufactures, and accessible microbrands, across Switzerland, Germany, Japan, China, Finland, Russia, the United States, the United Kingdom, and beyond.

What "Independent" Means Here

A brand qualifies for inclusion if it is genuinely independently owned and operates at a scale, and with a level of in-house manufacturing, consistent with an atelier rather than an industrial house. Three things disqualify a brand:

  1. Group ownership. A brand owned by Swatch Group, Richemont, LVMH, the Franck Muller Watchland Group, Fossil Group, Movado Group, Festina Group, Citychamp Watch & Jewellery Group, Timex Group, or Mondaine Group is excluded, regardless of how the brand presents itself.
  2. Industrial scale. A brand can be independently owned and still fall outside this catalogue if its revenue, production volume, or workforce places it among the industry's established major houses rather than its independent ateliers — Rolex, Patek Philippe, Audemars Piguet, Richard Mille, and Seiko/Grand Seiko among them.
  3. Homage-driven output. Brands whose catalogue is primarily close copies of other brands' existing designs, without meaningful original watchmaking, are excluded.

Exceptions, and Why They Exist

A handful of brands are included despite one of the above generally counting against them, because the underlying substance outweighs the surface-level exclusion criterion:

  • Christopher Ward and Yema produce at meaningfully larger volume than most of this catalogue, but both have invested seriously in genuine in-house manufacturing — Christopher Ward with its SH21 and CW-001–003 calibers and a GPHG-winning in-house chiming complication; Yema with four generations of in-house calibers, including an in-house tourbillon.
  • BALL Watch Company is included on the same basis: its broad catalogue runs predominantly on modified, COSC-certified ETA and Sellita movements, but since 2017 it has developed genuine manufacture calibers through its Patrick Labs technical division, reserved for a distinct Engineer M / Roadmaster M tier the brand markets as built completely in-house.
  • Arnold & Son, Angelus (Citizen Group, via La Joux-Perret) and Ferdinand Berthoud (Chopard) sit under larger corporate ownership but operate as genuinely autonomous manufactures, with their own premises, teams, and full creative and technical control over their own movements and designs.
  • Credor's Micro Artist Studio and Shizukuishi Watch Studio (Seiko) are a small circle of craftsmen, personally trained in finishing by Philippe Dufour, producing extremely limited pieces functioning in every practical sense like an independent atelier.

The full, current list of excluded groups and named exceptions is maintained on an ongoing basis as ownership structures change.

How a Listing Gets Written

Every entry is built from primary sources — a maker's own site, interviews, and published specifications — cross-checked against specialist watch press (Monochrome, SJX, Hodinkee, Revolution, Fratello, aBlogtoWatch, and others) and, where relevant, auction records and GPHG documentation. Listings are written as original prose, not copied or lightly reworded from any single source, and are held to a minimum standard of depth — typically three substantial paragraphs — covering a maker's history, their technical and design identity, and their current work. Where public information about a maker is genuinely limited, that limitation is stated honestly rather than padded with speculation.

Keeping the Catalogue Current

This is a living document. Listings are revised as brands release new work, win awards, change ownership, or as errors are caught — including, on occasion, correcting our own prior mistakes. New makers who meet the criteria above are added on an ongoing basis as they're discovered.

Corrections and Suggestions

Spotted an error, or think a watchmaker belongs here? Email indiewatchmaking@gmail.com — corrections are always welcome, and every suggestion is genuinely reviewed against the criteria above.

Editorial Independence

No brand can pay to be included in this catalogue, or to influence what its listing says. The description, history, and facts written for every maker are never for sale. Brands may in the future have the option to add supplementary features to their own listing, but editorial content itself remains independent.
