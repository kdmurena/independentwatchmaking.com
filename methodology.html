Methodology of Curation

How this catalogue is built, and how "independent" is defined.

IndependentWatchmaking.com is a free, independently curated reference documenting the full spectrum of independent horology worldwide — legendary masters, AHCI members, one-person artisan workshops, historic manufactures, and accessible microbrands, across Switzerland, Germany, Japan, China, Finland, Russia, the United States, the United Kingdom, and beyond.

What "Independent" Means Here

This catalogue is dedicated to independent watchmaking — makers and brands who set their own creative direction, answer to no distant parent company, and have relatively small output or income. To keep that focus meaningful, a number of well-known, exceptional watch brands have not been included.

A brand does not appear here if any of the following is true: (1) it is owned by one of the global luxury conglomerates that dominate the watch industry; (2) it is independently or family-owned but has grown into a large-scale or mass-market commercial operation whose revenue or production volume places it closer to an established industrial house than an independent atelier; or (3) its output consists primarily of homages or close copies of other brands' existing designs rather than original work.

Exceptions: Included Despite Group Ownership

A very small number of group-owned brands are included because they operate as genuinely autonomous manufactures — separate premises, separate teams, full creative and technical control over their own movements and designs, functioning in every practical sense like an independent house rather than a division of a larger commercial operation.

  • Arnold & Son and Angelus — owned by Citizen Group via Manufacture La Joux-Perret, but designing and producing their movements entirely in-house at their own La Chaux-de-Fonds manufacture.
  • Ferdinand Berthoud — owned by Chopard, but conceived and run as a dedicated independent manufacture in Fleurier with full creative and technical autonomy.
  • Credor's Micro Artist Studio & Shizukuishi Watch Studio — owned by Seiko, but two small, dedicated ateliers whose finishing techniques were personally taught by Philippe Dufour, producing extremely limited pieces (as few as eight) independently of Seiko's broader industrial production.

Exceptions: Included Despite Scale

A very small number of higher-volume brands are included because, despite producing at a scale closer to an established industrial house, they have invested seriously in genuine in-house manufacturing rather than simply assembling bought-in parts — engineering substance we believe still reflects independent creative and technical ambition, not commodity production.

  • Christopher Ward — produces at a considerably larger scale than most of this catalogue, but has developed multiple in-house calibers (SH21, CW-001–003) and won a 2023 GPHG award for its in-house Bel Canto chiming complication.
  • Yema — also produces at meaningfully larger volume, but has invested in four successive generations of in-house calibers since 2009, including an in-house tourbillon (the CMM.30) — a rare achievement at any price, let alone an accessible one.
  • BALL Watch Company — its broad catalogue runs predominantly on modified, COSC-certified ETA and Sellita movements, but since 2017 it has developed genuine manufacture calibers (Caliber 7309 and its successors) through its Patrick Labs technical division, reserved for a distinct Engineer M / Roadmaster M tier the brand markets as built completely in-house.

This list reflects our best current understanding of each brand's ownership and scale, and it will change as circumstances do — a brand that regains independence, or an independent that is acquired, will move accordingly. If you believe a listing here is outdated or incorrect, corrections are always welcome.

How a Listing Gets Written

Every entry is built from primary sources — a maker's own site, interviews, and published specifications — cross-checked against specialist watch press (Monochrome, SJX, Hodinkee, Revolution, Fratello, aBlogtoWatch, and others) and, where relevant, auction records and GPHG documentation. Listings are written as original prose, not copied or lightly reworded from any single source, and are held to a minimum standard of depth — typically three substantial paragraphs — covering a maker's history, their technical and design identity, and their current work. Where public information about a maker is genuinely limited, that limitation is stated honestly rather than padded with speculation.

Keeping the Catalogue Current

This is a living document. Listings are revised as brands release new work, win awards, change ownership, or as errors are caught — including, on occasion, correcting our own prior mistakes. New makers who meet the criteria above are added on an ongoing basis as they're discovered.

Corrections and Suggestions

Spotted an error, or think a watchmaker belongs here? Email indiewatchmaking@gmail.com — corrections are always welcome, and every suggestion is genuinely reviewed against the criteria above.

Editorial Independence

No brand can pay to be included in this catalogue, or to influence what its listing says. The description, history, and facts written for every maker are never for sale. Brands may in the future have the option to add supplementary features to their own listing, but editorial content itself remains independent.
